What's the realistic risk that the STR permit doesn't transfer at closing?
Scout · 2 days ago Generated in 8.4s

Low-to-moderate risk, with one specific hinge point. Here's how I read it:

Monterey County's STR program operates on a conditional use permit (CUP) tied to the property, not the owner. [1] That's structurally favorable — when the property transfers, the permit transfers with it, assuming nothing else has changed. No re-application required.

Permit type
Conditional use permit · property-bound · transfers at sale

The hinge point: Monterey County introduced an enforcement update in December 2025 requiring all CUP holders to re-attest annually that the property continues to meet conditions (no neighbor complaints, septic capacity, parking minimums). [2] The current owner attested in February 2026, so the next attestation isn't due until February 2027 — well after your projected closing.

What I'd verify in your inspection contingency window:

1. Pull the permit from the County Planning office and confirm no enforcement complaints filed in the last 12 months.
2. Confirm parking still meets minimums (2 off-street spaces) — your survey will show this.
3. Confirm septic system capacity rating matches occupancy on permit (sleeps 8). The 2014 permit application listed it; verify it's still valid.

Your closing timeline (June 6) puts you well clear of the next attestation. The permit transfer is the safe assumption — but the attestation pattern is worth knowing for the next renewal cycle.

If the inspection finds the septic isn't rated for sleeps-8, what's that going to cost to upgrade?
Scout · 2 days ago Generated in 12.1s

In Monterey County, septic capacity is calculated by bedroom count (not headcount), so the question is really whether the existing system was permitted for the property's 4 bedrooms. The county's standard is 1,000 gallons per bedroom for systems serving STR-zoned properties [3], so you'd want a system rated for at least 4,000 gallons.

Three scenarios, with cost ranges from comps in the area I checked:

Best case · system already 4,000+ gal
$0 · only need to confirm via inspection record from County Health
Likely case · capacity adequate but pump/d-box needs work
$3,500–$8,000 · pump replacement + distribution box · 1-2 day install
Worst case · full leach field replacement
$28,000–$45,000 · 4-6 weeks · permit required · Monterey County coastal zoning adds complexity

My recommendation: add a septic-specific inspection contingency ($550-$800 for a Coastal Septic certified inspector) on top of Mike Chen's general inspection. You'll get the permitted capacity record and a flow test in one visit. If the worst case shows up, you have grounds to renegotiate price by $20K+ or walk on contingency. Worth the $750.
